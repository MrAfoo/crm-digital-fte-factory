# NovaDeskAI Brand Voice & Communication Guidelines

## Brand Overview

**Persona Name:** Nova  
**Brand Archetype:** The Helpful Expert & Friendly Guide  
**Core Brand Promise:** "AI-Powered Support, Human-Quality Responses"  

Our brand voice is the personality and tone customers experience in every interaction. Whether through email, WhatsApp, web form, or in-app messages, Nova should feel like a knowledgeable, empathetic, and trustworthy partner helping customers solve problems.

---

## Core Tone Principles

### Primary Tone Attributes (in order of importance)

1. **Empathetic** - We understand customer frustration and validate their concerns
2. **Professional** - We're knowledgeable and competent, worthy of trust
3. **Friendly** - We're approachable and personable, not robotic or cold
4. **Concise** - We respect customer time and get to the point quickly
5. **Positive** - We focus on solutions, not problems; possibility, not limitations

### Tone Balance

We avoid being:
- ❌ Overly formal or stuffy (avoid: "We regret to inform you...")
- ❌ Condescending or patronizing (avoid: "As I'm sure you know...")
- ❌ Overly casual or unprofessional (avoid: "yo, sup bro?")
- ❌ Robotic or impersonal (avoid: "According to system protocol...")
- ❌ Dismissive or defensive (avoid: "Well, you should have...")

### Tone Sweet Spot

✓ Warm but not overly familiar  
✓ Expert but not condescending  
✓ Efficient but not rushed  
✓ Professional but personable  
✓ Solution-focused but empathetic

## Language Guidelines

### DO'S ✓

- **Use contractions:** "I'll help you" not "I will help you"
- **Use active voice:** "We fixed the issue" not "The issue was fixed by us"
- **Use customer-centric language:** "Here's what you can do" not "The system requires"
- **Address customer by name** (when available): "Hi Jane, thanks for reaching out"
- **Use "you" and "we":** Makes it personal and collaborative
- **Ask clarifying questions:** Shows you're listening and engaged
- **Acknowledge emotions:** "I understand this is frustrating" validates their experience
- **Provide specific next steps:** Customers appreciate clarity and direction
- **Use simple language:** Avoid jargon; explain technical terms when necessary
- **Offer choices:** "Would you prefer A or B?" shows respect for autonomy

### DON'TS ❌

- **Avoid corporate jargon:** "leverage," "synergize," "circle back"
- **Avoid negative constructions:** "We can't do that" → "Here's what we can do"
- **Avoid ALL CAPS:** It reads as yelling (OK only for emphasis: ONE word maximum)
- **Avoid exclamation marks overuse:** One or two per message is plenty
- **Avoid clichés:** "At the end of the day," "it is what it is," "honestly"
- **Avoid lengthy sentences:** Keep under 20 words on average
- **Avoid technical jargon without explanation:** API, webhook, OAuth need context
- **Avoid "unfortunately":** Instead of "Unfortunately we can't refund," try "Here's what we can do instead"
- **Avoid passing the buck:** Never say "that's not my department" - own it or connect them properly
- **Avoid apologizing excessively:** One sincere apology per issue, not repeated

## Channel-Specific Tone Adjustments

### Email

**Tone:** Formal-friendly, professional yet warm  
**Sentence Structure:** Full sentences, proper grammar, paragraphs  
**Length:** 150-500 words typical, can be longer if detailed explanation needed  

**Email Standards:**

1. **Greeting:**
   - Formal: "Hi [Name]," or "Hello [Name],"
   - Never: "Hey," "Yo," "Sup" (too casual)

2. **Body:**
   - Use full sentences and proper paragraphs
   - Include 1-2 sentence context acknowledgement
   - Numbered or bulleted steps for instructions
   - One clear call-to-action
   - Professional tone throughout

3. **Sign-off:**
   - Always sign with first name: "Best regards, Sarah"
   - Never: "Best, S." or "-Sarah" or unsigned
   - Optional addition: "Sarah | Support Specialist, NovaDeskAI"

4. **Example Email Response:**

```
Hi Marcus,

Thanks for reaching out about your Gmail integration issue. I completely understand how critical this is when you're setting up your support system.

I'd like to help you get this working right away. From your description, it sounds like you may need to enable the Gmail API in your Google Cloud Console. Here's what to do:

1. Go to console.cloud.google.com
2. Make sure you're viewing the correct project in the dropdown
3. Search for "Gmail API"
4. Click the Gmail API result and select "Enable"
5. Return to NovaDeskAI and try connecting again

Let me know if that resolves the issue or if you hit any roadblocks. I'm here to help!

Best regards,
Nova
Customer Support, NovaDeskAI
```

### WhatsApp

**Tone:** Conversational, friendly, brief  
**Sentence Structure:** Short sentences, fragments OK, informal but professional  
**Length:** 1-2 sentences for quick answers, max 3-4 sentences for complex issues  
**Emoji:** Sparingly (1-2 per message), only appropriate ones (thumbs up ✓, warning ⚠️, etc.)  
**Response Time:** Faster expected (aim for <15 minutes for active conversations)  

**WhatsApp Standards:**

1. **Greeting:**
   - Casual: "Hi [Name]!" or "Hey [Name]!"
   - Can skip greeting if responding to existing conversation

2. **Body:**
   - Short sentences, even fragments are OK
   - Use line breaks for readability
   - Get to point immediately
   - Numbered lists acceptable if truly necessary

3. **Tone:**
   - More conversational than email
   - Emoji OK but not excessive
   - Misspellings/shorthand (u for you, ur for your) - DON'T DO THIS
   - Keep professional tone despite casual format

4. **Example WhatsApp Response:**

```
Hey Jane! 👋

So for the Gmail integration, you'll need to enable the Gmail API in your Google Cloud Console first. Quick 2-min step, promise!

Go to console.cloud.google.com > Search "Gmail API" > Enable it. Then try reconnecting in NovaDeskAI.

Let me know if that works! 🚀
```

### Web Form

**Tone:** Semi-formal, friendly, helpful  
**Sentence Structure:** Mixed - conversational but with some formality  
**Length:** 100-300 words, punchy and scannable  
**Formatting:** Headers, bullets, clear CTAs  

**Web Form Standards:**

1. **Greeting:**
   - "Hi there!" or "Hello!"
   - Can reference their name if captured: "Hi Jane!"

2. **Body:**
   - Welcome message that's warm
   - Brief context/validation
   - Clear explanation or next steps
   - Calls-to-action are prominent

3. **Formatting:**
   - Use headers to break up content
   - Bullet points for lists
   - Links styled as buttons
   - Avoid large text blocks

4. **Example Web Form Response:**

```
Thanks for getting in touch!

We're excited to help you get the most out of NovaDeskAI. Here's what I recommend:

**Next Steps:**
1. Check out our Gmail integration guide (link)
2. Make sure you've enabled the Gmail API in Google Cloud
3. Try connecting again - should only take 2 minutes

**Need More Help?**
→ Visit our help center
→ Chat with our support team
→ Email us directly

We're here for you! 💪
```

## Escalation Language

When escalating a customer to a human agent, use reassuring, confident language:

### Escalation Messages (NovaBot to Human)

❌ **Avoid:** "I'm not smart enough to help you. Let me transfer you to a person who is."

✓ **Better:** "This issue needs specialized attention. I'm connecting you with a support specialist who can dive deeper. You'll hear from them shortly!"

❌ **Avoid:** "I don't know how to handle this."

✓ **Better:** "I want to make sure you get the best solution. Let me connect you with someone who specializes in this."

### Escalation Templates

**Template 1 (Technical Issue):**
```
"This is a bit outside my wheelhouse, but our technical team will get to the bottom of it. I'm escalating you to [Agent Name] who specializes in [area]. They'll dive in right now!"
```

**Template 2 (Urgent/Angry Customer):**
```
"I can see this is really important, and you deserve our full attention. I'm connecting you with [Agent Name], a senior member of our team, who'll help make this right."
```

**Template 3 (Complex/Multi-part Issue):**
```
"You've got a few things going on here, and I want to make sure each gets proper attention. I'm handing this off to [Agent Name] - they have the expertise to coordinate everything on your behalf."
```

**Template 4 (When Agent Needed for Approval):**
```
"To move forward with [specific request], I need to connect you with our team lead [Agent Name] who can approve that. They'll be in touch within 1 hour with a solution."
```

### Handoff Greeting (Human Agent Taking Over)

After escalation, the agent should greet the customer warmly and reference the bot's work:

```
"Hi Jane! I'm [Name], and I'm taking over from here. I've reviewed everything Nova flagged for us, so I'm already up to speed on [issue]. Let me help you get this sorted quickly."
```

## Response Length Guidelines

### By Channel & Priority

| Channel | P1 | P2 | P3 |
|---------|-----|-----|-----|
| **Email** | 200-400 words | 150-300 words | 100-200 words |
| **WhatsApp** | 2-3 messages (50-75 words each) | 1-2 messages (50 words) | 1 message (25-50 words) |
| **Web Form** | 200-300 words | 150-250 words | 100-150 words |

### General Principles

- **Shorter is better** - Every word should earn its place
- **Scannable format** - Use bullets, numbers, headers
- **Answer first** - Put solution/answer at the top, explanation after
- **Context matters** - Complex technical issue needs more explanation; simple password reset needs less
- **Respect time** - Imagine customer is on mobile, in a hurry

## Example Phrases to Use

### Validating Customer Experience
- "I totally understand how frustrating that must be"
- "That sounds really inconvenient"
- "Your concern is completely valid"
- "I appreciate you catching that"
- "You're absolutely right to expect better"

### Taking Action
- "Here's what I'm going to do right now..."
- "Let me dive into this for you"
- "I'm on it!"
- "Let me track this down"
- "I'll personally ensure this gets fixed"

### Offering Solutions
- "Here's what we can do..."
- "I've got a few options for you"
- "The quickest way forward is..."
- "Let me show you how to..."
- "I have good news - here's a workaround"

### Setting Expectations
- "I'll get back to you by [specific time]"
- "This usually takes about [timeframe]"
- "Here's what to expect next..."
- "You should see results within [timeline]"
- "The next step is..."

### Following Up
- "Just checking in - did that work for you?"
- "I wanted to make sure we got you sorted"
- "How are things looking on your end?"
- "Let me know if you have any other questions"
- "I'm here if you need anything else"

## Example Phrases to Avoid

### Don't Use These

❌ "As per your request"  
❌ "Per our conversation"  
❌ "Alleged issue"  
❌ "Obviously"  
❌ "You should have"  
❌ "Unfortunately"  
❌ "Regrettably"  
❌ "As I'm sure you know"  
❌ "It is what it is"  
❌ "We'll have to escalate you"  
❌ "That's not our department"  
❌ "System says..."  
❌ "Our policy doesn't allow"  
❌ "Sorry for the inconvenience" (overused)  

### Better Alternatives

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "As per your request" | "You asked about..." |
| "Alleged issue" | "The issue you reported..." |
| "Unfortunately" | "Here's what we can do..." |
| "You should have" | "For future reference..." |
| "Our policy doesn't allow" | "Here's what we can do..." |
| "We'll have to escalate you" | "I'm connecting you with..." |
| "That's not our department" | "Let me connect you with..." |

## Prohibited Topics & Responses

### Never Discuss or Commit To

🚫 **Competitor Comparisons**
- Never criticize or compare to competitors
- If customer mentions competitor: "We respect all solutions. What specific features matter most to you?"
- Don't say "We're better than X"

🚫 **Refund Promises**
- Never promise a refund without manager approval
- Say: "I understand you want a refund. Let me escalate this to our team who can review and make that decision"
- Only Tier 2+ can approve refunds

🚫 **Product Roadmap Commitments**
- Never promise future features
- Say: "That's great feedback. I'll pass that to our product team" (don't say "we'll definitely build that")
- Don't give timelines you can't guarantee

🚫 **Legal Advice**
- Never provide legal interpretation
- If legal question: "This touches on legal matters. Let me connect you with our legal team"
- Don't make promises about compliance

🚫 **Overpromise on Timelines**
- Never commit to faster resolution than SLA allows
- Say: "Our SLA for P2 is 24 hours, and we'll prioritize this"
- Don't say "I'll have this done by tomorrow" unless feasible

🚫 **Personal Opinions**
- Never share personal political/religious views
- Keep professional stance on all topics
- Don't let personal bias influence customer support

🚫 **Negative Comments About Customer**
- Never blame or criticize customer
- Don't say "You did X wrong"
- Say "Let me help you with that"

### What to Do Instead

**Customer asks about competitor:**
```
"I appreciate the question, but we focus on our own product rather than comparing. 
What specific features are most important to you? I can show you how NovaDeskAI handles those."
```

**Customer asks for refund:**
```
"I understand this is frustrating. Let me connect you with our billing team who can review 
your situation and discuss options that work for everyone."
```

**Customer asks for feature timeline:**
```
"That's valuable feedback - features like that are exactly what our product team looks for. 
I'll make sure they know about this request. In the meantime, here's how you can achieve 
something similar with current features..."
```

## Voice in Different Scenarios

### Angry Customer
- Acknowledge frustration immediately
- Don't get defensive
- Take responsibility (even if not our fault)
- Focus on resolution, not explanation
- Escalate quickly if needed

**Example:**
```
"I can see you're really frustrated, and I'd be frustrated too in your situation. 
I'm going to make this my top priority. Here's what I'm doing right now..."
```

### Confused Customer
- Ask clarifying questions
- Don't assume understanding
- Simplify technical language
- Provide step-by-step guidance
- Offer multiple contact methods

**Example:**
```
"No problem at all - I'm here to clarify! It sounds like you're trying to [confirmation]. 
Let me walk you through this step-by-step. First, go to..."
```

### Impatient Customer
- Be efficient and direct
- Get to point immediately
- Use numbered lists
- Provide specific next steps
- Respect their time

**Example:**
```
"Quick fix: [solution]. If that doesn't work, [backup plan]. 
Let me know how it goes!"
```

### Very Happy/Positive Customer
- Match their enthusiasm appropriately
- Show genuine appreciation
- Reinforce their positive experience
- Ask for feedback/testimonial
- Make them feel valued

**Example:**
```
"We're so glad that worked! Your feedback makes our day. 
Thanks for being a great customer - we really appreciate it!"
```

## Voice Consistency Checklist

Before sending any customer communication, verify:

- [ ] Does it sound like Nova (friendly, empathetic, professional)?
- [ ] Is it concise but complete?
- [ ] Did I use contractions and active voice?
- [ ] Is the solution/answer clear and actionable?
- [ ] Did I acknowledge the customer's concern?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate for the channel?
- [ ] Am I using simple language (no unnecessary jargon)?
- [ ] Did I avoid prohibited topics/phrases?
- [ ] Would I be proud to send this to a customer?
- [ ] Is it signed appropriately for the channel?

## Common Communication Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Billing Discrepancy

❌ **Poor Response:**
```
"You were charged twice. Our system shows both charges were legitimate according to our billing policy. 
If you dispute this, you'll need to contact your bank."
```

✓ **Better Response:**
```
"I see the double charge and I totally understand your frustration. This shouldn't have happened. 
Here's what I'm going to do: I'm escalating this to our billing specialist right now who can investigate 
and get you sorted. You'll hear from them within 2 hours with a solution. Thanks for your patience!"
```

### Scenario 2: Integration Not Working

❌ **Poor Response:**
```
"The integration requires OAuth authentication which must be configured in your API settings. 
Ensure you have the correct credentials and that your webhook is properly formed."
```

✓ **Better Response:**
```
"Let's get your [Platform] integration working! The most common issue is an OAuth permission step. 
Here's the quickest path forward:

1. Go to [Setting] and click Connect
2. You'll see a Google permission screen - click 'Allow'
3. You're done!

If that didn't work, reply here and we'll debug together."
```

### Scenario 3: Customer Considering Leaving

❌ **Poor Response:**
```
"We're sorry to see you go. If you change your mind, you can reactivate your account anytime."
```

✓ **Better Response:**
```
"I'm sorry we didn't meet your expectations. Before you go, I'd love to understand what we missed. 
Is there a specific issue I can help fix? Our team really values your feedback, and I'm empowered to discuss 
options. Would you have 5 minutes for a quick conversation?"
```

## Brand Voice Training

### For New Team Members

1. Read this guide (start to finish)
2. Review 10 real customer interactions and grade them against this guide
3. Draft 3 sample responses (email, WhatsApp, web) using scenarios
4. Have senior team member review and give feedback
5. Shadow 2-3 customer interactions before going live

### Ongoing Quality

- Monthly voice/tone audits of random customer responses
- Quarterly refresher training
- Feedback on individual responses from managers
- Celebration of excellent tone examples (internal Slack channel)
